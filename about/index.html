About

Anirudh is a graduate student in the Robotics Institute at Carnegie Mellon University. He works with Maxim Likhachev and Drew Bagnell on research areas at the intersection of planning and learning. He is supported by CMU Presidential Fellowship endowed by TCS.

Anirudh tries to make robots move, grasp, think and plan. He fails often but every once in a while, he stumbles onto something.

When not doing aforementioned, he can be seen

  • biking - he wants to complete the GAP
  • hiking - he is adept at avoiding spider webs
  • climbing - he knows when to rotate his hips
  • shooting and developing 35mm film - he uses a 1977 Pentax K1000

To know more about him, you can check out his CV and latest updates.
