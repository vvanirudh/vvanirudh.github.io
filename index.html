Anirudh Vemula

Senior Software Engineer @Aurora. Previously, PhD in Robotics @CMU, CS @IITB

Anirudh is a senior software engineer at Aurora in Pittsburgh. He is making trucks go vroooom on their own.

In the past, Anirudh was a graduate student in the Robotics Institute at Carnegie Mellon University. He worked with Maxim Likhachev and Drew Bagnell on research areas at the intersection of planning and learning. He was supported by CMU Presidential Fellowship. You can find his thesis here.

Anirudh tries to make robots move, grasp, think and plan. He fails often but every once in a while, he stumbles onto something.

When not doing aforementioned, he can be seen

  • biking - he has completed the GAP and C&O bike trail from Pittsburgh to DC (333.3 miles)
  • hiking - he is adept at avoiding spider webs
  • climbing - he knows when to rotate his hips
  • shooting and developing 35mm film - he uses a 1977 Pentax K1000
